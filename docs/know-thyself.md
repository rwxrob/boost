# Know thyself

Get a journal, perhaps go for a walk, and really think about the following before writing your answers so that you can review them later. You can always change them as you discover more about yourself.

>[!IMPORTANT]
>Be brutally honest. Turn off all the programming and scripts and expectations from others you have running in your head. This isn't about who you are _expected_ to be. It's about discovering who you actually are. The sooner you discover this, the sooner you can get busy making every decision in the service of that goal (advice from "The Naked Man" in How I Met Your Father).

## Are your basic human needs met?

It's impossible to work on your "best self" and think about your dream job if you don't know where the next meal for you or your children is going to come from or how you are going to pay for rent next month. There's something called _Maslov's Heirarchy of Needs_ which addresses this very thing. It's a waste of time focusing on _anything_ else until these basic needs are met. Consider these questions first.

- Did I eat enough today to stay healthy?
- Am I living in a place where shelter from the elements is guaranteed for the next year?
- Do I have access to a place to use a computer and learn in a safe environment?

If your answer to any of these is no then take immediate steps to make all the answers yes. Consider the following:

- Move in with a family member or friend temporarily
- Get a part-time service job to pay the minimal rent you need

## What do _you_ like to do?

- What activities do I really enjoy?
- What would I be shocked to learn someone would pay me to do?
- What are my hobbies and would I want to be paid for them?

Be as general or specific as you feel like.

- "I like helping others learn."
- "I love typing."
- "I cannot learn enough about people and their stories."
- "I can't be in an office."

If you struggle with what you like start with what you don't like.

- "I hate working with kids." (Had a teacher once realize this after year of teaching.)
- "I cannot deal with office politics."

## What am I naturally good at?

- What have sincere people told me I'm good at in the past?
