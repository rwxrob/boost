## Why go in person

Most of what matters at a conference does not happen in the sessions. It happens in the hallway, at lunch, at the evening events, and in the five-minute conversation after a talk where the speaker is still at the podium. You cannot replicate that online.

Going in person means:

- Meeting the people whose blog posts and GitHub commits you have been reading
- Seeing what companies are actually hiring for right now, not six months ago
- Getting a feel for what is hype versus what is being run in production at scale
- Making yourself visible and memorable to potential employers and collaborators

If you are early in your career, treat every conference as a job fair. Wear comfortable shoes, bring business cards or a QR code to your GitHub, and talk to everyone.

## CFP (Call for Proposals)

If you have something worth sharing, submit a talk. You do not need to be famous. You need a clear idea, a real story, and the willingness to stand up and share it. Speaking gets you a free or discounted ticket, puts your name in the program, and opens doors that passive attendance does not.

CFPs for KubeCon typically open six to nine months before the event. Watch the CNCF mailing list or the event pages for announcements.
