# Getting into tech with AI: still worth it?

There's a reasonable question floating around right now: if AI can write code, answer questions, and automate tasks, why would anyone bother learning tech from scratch?

It's a fair thing to wonder. But the answer, once you understand what's actually happening in the industry, is clear: AI makes getting into tech *more* accessible than ever — not less necessary.

## AI doesn't replace the need for tech workers

AI is a tool. Like every other tool in history — the spreadsheet, the IDE, the cloud — it amplifies what skilled workers can do, and it raises the floor for what unskilled workers can produce.

What it doesn't do is eliminate the need for humans who understand what they're building, why, and whether it's working.

Every AI output needs someone to evaluate it. Every system needs someone to deploy, monitor, and debug it. Every project needs someone who can look at AI-generated code and say "this is wrong" or "this won't scale." That person is a tech worker. They are more valuable now, not less.

## The jobs have changed, not disappeared

The nature of entry-level tech work is shifting fast. Tasks that used to take a junior developer a week — scaffolding a project, writing boilerplate, basic CRUD endpoints — an AI can now sketch out in minutes.

That's not a reason to despair. It means the bar has shifted. You don't need to be able to write everything from scratch. You need to be able to:

- Give AI clear, correct instructions
- Read and verify what it produces
- Debug when something goes wrong
- Understand the system well enough to make good decisions

These are learnable skills. The Boost teaches exactly these skills.

## You still need foundations

Here's what AI cannot do for you: build the mental model that lets you spot a bad answer.

If you don't know what a database index is, you can't tell when AI skips one and causes a performance disaster. If you don't understand how HTTP works, you can't debug a broken API. If you don't know what a process is, you can't understand why your container keeps crashing.

AI is a force multiplier. You still have to bring force.

The Boost exists to give you those foundations — not the old-school "memorize every syntax" kind, but the "understand what's actually happening" kind that makes you useful with or without AI.

## The agentic era rewards people who understand terminals and systems

There's a new category of work emerging: agentic development. Running multiple AI agents, giving them tasks, reviewing their output, stitching together workflows. This is real work that pays well and that almost nobody knows how to do yet.

The infrastructure for this work now has an open, neutral home: the [Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF)](https://aaif.io), a Linux Foundation project launched in December 2025. Its founding projects — Model Context Protocol (MCP), goose, and AGENTS.md — are already the de facto standards for connecting AI agents to tools, running local agent workflows, and giving agents consistent project instructions. Backing comes from AWS, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, and others. This is the open source layer that agentic AI is being built on, the same way Linux and Kubernetes are the layer that cloud infrastructure is built on.

Knowing the AAIF exists and following its projects puts you ahead of the large majority of people entering tech right now. Attending AAIF events like AGNTCon and MCP Dev Summits is how you show employers you are not just learning about this space — you are in it.

It looks a lot like what this Boost teaches. You need to know:

- How to use a terminal
- How git works
- How to read logs and error messages
- How to reason about systems

People who have these skills and can work alongside AI are in the best position of anyone in the industry right now.

## The opportunity is real but narrow

Not everyone who "learns to code" will get a job. That's always been true and it's still true. But the people who combine genuine understanding of how systems work with fluency in AI tools are genuinely hard to find right now.

That gap won't last forever. This is a window. The Boost helps you climb through it.

## The bottom line

Learn the foundations. Use AI as your pair programmer, your tutor, and your force multiplier. Build things. Show your work on GitHub. Get comfortable in the terminal. Understand what you're building well enough to fix it when it breaks.

That's what the Boost is for. And no, AI hasn't made it irrelevant. If anything, it's made it more urgent.
