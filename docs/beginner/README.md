# Tech Career Boost: Beginner Track

This track takes you from "Am I interested in tech?" to "I'm ready to build as a technologist." Whether you become an employee, founder, or solo IT expert, the foundation is the same.

> [!CRITICAL]
> Start with Phase 1. Knowing yourself saves hundreds of thousands in wasted education, wrong degree programs, and years of frustration on the wrong path. Do not skip it.

## Phase 1: Know yourself, explore, and decide

Becoming a successful technologist is more like becoming a doctor now than before. AI has rendered junior tech positions virtually nonexistent. You must understand yourself deeply — your strengths, passions, and boundaries — because there's no "junior developer" rung on the ladder anymore. You go from learning to contributing at a mid-level immediately.

Before you learn anything, understand yourself and whether tech is actually for you. Getting this right now saves enormous amounts of time, money, and energy.

- [Know thyself](know-thyself.md) — **Start here.** Evaluate your skill stack: What are you actually good at? What have others told you you're good at? What specific activities bring you joy and you'd want to do eight hours a day? What do you absolutely *not* want to do? (Example: "I do not want to work with children, even though I'm into education.") This clarity is your foundation — it will save you hundreds of thousands in misdirected education and career changes.
- [Are there still jobs in tech?](../shared/are-there-still-jobs-in-tech.md) — cold reality of the current market and what roles actually exist
- [Getting into tech with AI: still worth it?](getting-into-tech-with-ai.md) — why now is still a good time, even with AI in the mix
- [What does a technologist actually do?](../shared/conferences-and-job-fairs.md) — attend events, talk to real engineers, don't guess

Use AI (ChatGPT, web search, Copilot) to *research* the industry, not to code yet. Build context before you build skills. Know *where you want to go* before you start the journey.
